# HR-Salary-Promotion-Prediction-Analysis
Analyzes employee salary growth, performance impact, and promotion timelines using Excel. Identifies key drivers of salary hike, highlights promotion gaps, and applies correlation and regression to generate data-driven compensation and workforce.
# Executive Summary
The organization lacked visibility into how salary hikes,performance ratings,and promotion timelines influence employee compensation and career progression.This project analyzed HR data to predict salary growth, identify promotion gaps,and evaluate the relationship between performance and salary increments. The analysis revealed a consistent increase in predicted salary following salary hikes, a strong positive correlation (0.773) between performance and salary hike percentage, and a small group of employees with significantly delayed promotions. These insights highlight opportunities to improve compensation fairness and promotion strategies,potentially increasing employee satisfaction and retention.Future work will focus on refining predictive models and expanding variables to improve accuracy.
# Business Problem
The organisation has employee salary and performance data but lacks clear insight into:
How salary hikes impact employee earnings over time
Whether performance ratings are effectively linked to compensation
Which employees are at risk due to delayed promotions
How to predict future salary growth and promotion likelihood
# Methodology
Conducted data analysis using Excel to evaluate salary,performance, and promotion variables
Built a scatter plot with a linear forecast to analyze predicted salary after salary hike %
 Created a column chart to evaluate promotion likelihood against years since last promotion
 Performed correlation analysis to measure the relationship between performance rating and salary hike %
 Deveeloped a linear regression model to predict salary hike % based on performance rating

# Business Recommendations
Strengthen performance based compensation policies to ensure consistent and transparent salary inrements
Review promotion policies,especially for employees with long stagnation periods, to reduce retention risk
 Implement targeted promotion strategies in Sales and R&D departments where delays are evident
 Regularly monitor the relationship between performance and salary adjustments to maintain fairness and motivation
 Use predictive insights to proactively identify employees at risk of disengagement due to slow career progression
